# Prima Materia — The Raw Material

> The prima materia is not a problem to be solved. It is a condition to be transmuted.

---

Nondominium's Work begins with a clear-eyed identification of what it is dissolving. The raw material has four dimensions. Each is a distinct form of the same underlying pathology: governance that lives outside the resource it governs.

---

## The Four Dimensions

### 1. The Captured Resource

Today's shared resources require platform trust, centralized rules, and institutional authority to function. The platform IS the governance. If the platform goes away — the tool-sharing network shuts down, the equipment library changes its terms, the collaborative project moves to a hostile host — the commons it maintained disappears with it.

Communities building on top of platforms do not govern their shared resources. They lease the governance from whoever controls the platform. This is the fundamental capture: the governance does not belong to the community; it belongs to the intermediary.

Nondominium dissolves this by moving the governance into the resource. A ResourceSpecification carries its own access rules, transfer conditions, maintenance obligations, and end-of-life protocols. The platform becomes irrelevant because the rules travel with the resource.

### 2. The Trust Overhead Problem

Communities spend enormous energy on negotiation, monitoring, and enforcement because governance is external to the resources being governed. Every transaction requires trust that must be manually established, maintained, and renewed. Who can borrow this? Under what conditions? Who verifies return? Who tracks the history? Who resolves disputes?

This overhead is not a moral failing of communities — it is the structural consequence of governance living outside the resource. When the rules are not embedded, they must be continuously re-enacted by human coordination.

Nondominium dissolves this through the PPR system: Private Participation Receipts automate the trust record. Every Commitment-Claim-Event cycle generates exactly two cryptographically-signed receipts. The history accumulates automatically. The reputation emerges from the record without requiring any administrator to maintain it.

### 3. The Capture Failure Mode

When the platform disappears, changes its terms, or is seized by hostile actors, the commons it hosted disappears with it. This is not a hypothetical. Tool-sharing platforms have failed. Community networks have been acquired and enshittified. Institutional databases have been discontinued. The failure mode is well-documented and recurring.

The capture failure mode reveals that platform-dependent governance is not governance — it is tenancy. The community holds a lease, not a deed.

Nondominium dissolves this by making the resource itself the locus of governance. A resource with embedded governance rules does not require the original host to survive. It requires only that some Holochain node continues to hold the data — which, in an agent-centric network, is any of the agents who have interacted with it.

### 4. The Atomized Agent

Individuals cannot demonstrate their track record across contexts without relying on centralized reputation systems they do not control. LinkedIn owns your professional history. Airbnb owns your hosting reputation. Uber owns your driver rating. Each platform captures a slice of your economic identity and holds it hostage to their continued existence and goodwill.

When an agent moves between contexts — joining a new tool-sharing network, contributing to a new commons project, taking on a new stewardship role — they cannot carry their reputation with them. They start from zero, or they surrender to another platform.

Nondominium dissolves this through agent-controlled cryptographic reputation. PPRs are stored in the agent's own source chain as private entries. The agent controls what to share. The cryptographic signatures ensure authenticity without requiring the original platform. The reputation belongs to the agent, not the platform that witnessed it.

---

## The Transmutation

The four dimensions of the prima materia map to four acts of transmutation:

| Prima Materia | Transmutation |
|---------------|--------------|
| The captured resource | Governance embedded in ResourceSpecification |
| The trust overhead problem | Automated accountability via PPR system |
| The capture failure mode | Platform-independent resources via DHT |
| The atomized agent | Agent-controlled cryptographic reputation |

The Work is complete for a given community when all four transmutations are operational in their deployment. Until then, the Nigredo continues.
