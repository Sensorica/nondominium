# Mission

---

## Core Mission

Build the distributed infrastructure through which communities can govern shared resources without surrendering sovereignty to any platform, organization, or authority.

---

## Why It Matters

The commons has always existed. Communities have always shared tools, space, knowledge, and infrastructure. What has failed — repeatedly — is the governance infrastructure for those commons.

When governance lives in a platform, the commons is a lease. The platform sets the terms. The platform can change them. The platform can fail, be acquired, be weaponized. Every commons built on a platform is one board decision away from enclosure.

The current alternatives are worse: informal governance (trust-based, opaque, unscalable) and institutional governance (rigid, slow, requires professional administrators). Neither scales to the diversity and dynamism of a genuine peer-sharing economy.

Nondominium's answer is architectural: put the governance inside the resource. Make the rules machine-readable, embedded, and portable. Let the community define how their resource is accessed, transferred, maintained, and eventually retired — and then let those rules enforce themselves across any network configuration, any agent combination, any time horizon.

---

## Three Technical Pillars

**Embedded Governance** — Governance rules live inside the ResourceSpecification, not in application code or platform servers. Every instance of a resource inherits its governance rules. Rules enforce themselves through the Holochain validation system, not through administrative oversight.

**Privacy-Preserving Reputation** — Private Participation Receipts give agents a cryptographic reputation they control. Every validated economic interaction generates two signed receipts — one for each party. The history accumulates in the agent's own source chain. Reputation can be selectively disclosed without surrendering the underlying private record to any platform.

**Agent Sovereignty** — Each agent runs their own node. Each agent holds their own data. Each agent earns their own capabilities through validated participation, not through administrative assignment. The network provides coordination; it does not provide control.

---

## Position in the Technology Trinity

Nondominium is the governance layer of a three-part commons infrastructure:

1. **hAppenings (Requests & Offers)** — exchange coordination: what communities need and offer
2. **Nondominium** — governance: how communities manage shared resources
3. **IDI (Intelligent Didactic Interface)** — learning: how individuals develop within communities

Together these three systems provide complete commons infrastructure: the ability to coordinate needs, govern resources, and support individual development — without requiring any central authority for any of the three functions.

## The Sensorica Context

Nondominium is being built for and with Sensorica — one of the longest-running Open Value Network experiments in the world. Sensorica has been operating as a commons-based peer production network since 2011: contributors log value, governance is distributed, rewards flow proportionally.

The technical challenge Sensorica presents is real: how do you govern shared equipment, shared knowledge, and shared infrastructure across a distributed network of contributors who have never all met? How do you track who borrowed what, under what conditions, and whether they honored their commitments — without a central administrator?

Nondominium is the answer Sensorica needs and the one the broader commons ecosystem can use.

### The PEP Master Validation Case

The clearest validation of Nondominium's design is the PEP Master project: a collaborative open-source medical device for cystic fibrosis therapy developed jointly by Sensorica, Sainte Justine Children's Hospital, and Breathing Games. Three organizations, one shared resource (the device design and production infrastructure), a critical need for provenance tracking and contribution accounting.

This is not a toy use case. It is the hardest version of the governance problem: medical device provenance, multi-organizational contribution tracking, regulatory accountability, and the need to ensure that no single party can capture the commons they jointly produce.

If Nondominium works for PEP Master, it works.
