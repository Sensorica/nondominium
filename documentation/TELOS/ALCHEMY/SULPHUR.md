# Sulphur — The Essential Fire

> What Nondominium would pursue even if the entire technology stack changed.

---

*This file holds the project's declaration. Holochain is Mercury. Rust is Mercury. ValueFlows is Mercury — a medium through which the fire manifests. The Sulphur beneath all of it is something more fundamental.*

---

## The First Principle

A resource governed by rules it carries cannot be captured by any platform, authority, or actor.

This is the essential fire. Not the implementation. Not the language. Not the framework. The conviction that the correct place for governance is inside the resource itself — and that a resource carrying its own rules becomes, by definition, ungovernable by any single external actor.

Even if tomorrow Holochain ceased to exist and libp2p replaced it, even if Rust gave way to another systems language, even if ValueFlows vocabulary evolved into something unrecognizable — the Sulphur would remain: embedded governance as the structural antidote to platform capture.

---

## The Three Fires

**1. Capture Resistance**

No agent or group can monopolize a Nondominium resource. The governance rules embedded in each ResourceSpecification are enforced by the network, not by a platform that could be bought, seized, or corrupted. Capture resistance is not a feature added on top of the design — it is the design.

**2. Embedded Sovereignty**

Rules travel with the resource. When a community embeds its governance into a ResourceSpecification, those rules follow every instance of that resource across every context, every agent, every network hop. The governance does not live on a server that can be turned off. It lives in the structure that gives the resource its identity.

**3. Commons Restoration**

For centuries the commons has been enclosed — first by physical force, now by platform lock-in. Nondominium participates in the restoration of genuine commons governance: the condition where a community governs its shared resources without surrendering authority to any third party. Every resource deployed with embedded governance rules is a small act of enclosure reversed.

## What Is Mercury (Not Sulphur)

The following are mediums of expression — valuable, chosen carefully, but not the fire itself:

- The specific HDK version (0.5.3 today; 1.x tomorrow)
- The Rust language and WASM compilation target
- Svelte 5 as the frontend framework
- Tryorama as the testing framework
- ValueFlows as the economic vocabulary
- The Holochain DHT as the distribution mechanism
- The three-zome architecture (person / resource / gouvernance)

These are Mercury: the structural substance through which Sulphur manifests. They were chosen because they are currently the best available medium for the essential fire. They are not the fire.

---

## What the Community Must Never Surrender

- **The embedded governance rule**: any architectural move that externalizes governance — putting the rules in the application server rather than the resource — violates the first principle.
- **The capture resistance property**: any design that introduces a single point of control over resource access or governance decisions extinguishes the fire.
- **The agent's sovereignty over their own data**: any schema that centralizes private data or forces trust into a mediator betrays the commons restoration fire.
