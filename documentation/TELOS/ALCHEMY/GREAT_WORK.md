## The Prima Materia

The raw material: economic resources currently imprisoned in platform-dependent governance. Communities cannot share tools, equipment, knowledge, or infrastructure without surrendering authority over the rules of sharing to a platform, institution, or intermediary.

The prima materia is available everywhere. The lack is not shared resources — communities have always shared. The lack is the right process for governing that sharing without creating new dependencies.

---

## The Three Stages

### Nigredo — Dissolution (Current Work)

*The prima materia must be dissolved before it can be purified. The old form — governance as external enforcement — must be broken down.*

The Nigredo work is the construction of the dissolution apparatus:

- **zome_person**: Establishing agent identity and progressive capability (the trustable actor)
- **zome_resource**: Modeling economic resources with embedded governance rules (the governed object)
- **zome_gouvernance**: The operator that evaluates, enforces, and records (the dissolving agent)
- **PPR System**: Automating the reputation trail that replaces centralized trust records
- **ValueFlows Compliance**: Installing the shared economic grammar across all data structures

The Nigredo is not comfortable. The governance logic is complex, the cross-zome communication requires careful capability management, the test reliability demands patience. This is the nature of dissolution: the old form does not give way easily.

Current marker: Phase 2 complete, Phase 3 beginning.

### Albedo — Purification (Emerging)

*The essential substance separated from the dross. What remains is pure.*

The Albedo begins when Nondominium's core patterns have proven themselves and begin to propagate:

- **hREA Integration**: Nondominium's specialized governance layer connects to the broader ValueFlows network through hREA as the backend economic engine. The two systems are revealed as complementary, not competing.
- **PPR as Standard**: The Private Participation Receipt becomes a recognized pattern for privacy-preserving reputation across the Holochain ecosystem — not just a Nondominium feature.
- **Moss Deployment**: Real communities using Nondominium as infrastructure within the Moss ecosystem. The proof of concept becomes a production deployment.
- **TrueCommons Layer**: Higher-level peer production built on Nondominium's foundation — the first evidence that the foundation can support a cathedral.

The Albedo marker: when communities other than Sensorica adopt the system without prompting.

### Rubedo — Completion (The Vision)

*The Philosopher's Stone: the pattern that can replicate itself.*

- **Invisible Infrastructure**: Communities use Nondominium the way they use TCP/IP — without thinking about it. The governance rules are embedded. The reputation flows automatically. The coordination emerges from the structure.
- **Ecosystem Propagation**: The governance-as-operator pattern appears in projects built by people who have never met Nondominium's original authors. It has become part of the Holochain design vocabulary.
- **Self-Governing Commons**: Resources that govern themselves completely. No platform dependency. No institutional authority required. The community's governance rules, embedded at creation, enforcing themselves forever.

## The Philosopher's Stone

What "done" looks like at the largest scale:

When a community Nondominium's original authors have never met deploys the system, embeds their own governance rules into their ResourceSpecifications, runs their economic processes, earns their PPR reputation — and never needs to contact any authority, ask any administrator, or trust any platform — the Work is complete at that node.

The Stone is not a single deployment. It is not even many deployments. The Stone is the moment the governance-as-operator pattern becomes self-replicating: when the design insight propagates through the commons infrastructure ecosystem the way a good algorithm propagates through programming culture.

*The stone is not the resource. The stone is what makes ungovernable resources possible.*
